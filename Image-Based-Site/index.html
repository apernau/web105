My Life

in moments

ashleyrae

Growing up in Chicago has a way of altering your personality. You grow up loving all kinds of eclectic foods and learning about different cultures all within your neighborhood. I loved having the opportunity to learn about new things all within minutes of my childhood home. Chicago was a mecca for various ethnicities, religions, cultures, backgrounds, and more. It was eye-opening to be able to take all of it in starting at a young age, and it has molded me into the person I am today.

As a kid, I did not grow up lavishly. My family was middle working class, and we struggled from time to time just affording necessities. Growing up in a simple-minded home comes with its own challenges, but those challenges have helped me stay humble. I learned how to be self-sufficient at a very early age, and immediately took to building a stable and reliable work ethic around it. I started work early in my teens at a neighborhood youth center, which impacted the person I am today. It grounded me into a determined and motivated individual with the never-ending ability to adapt to whatever the circumstance provided me. I can say that I would not have the work ethic I have today without this place giving me the steppingstones to reach my full potential.

My work history had only begun to blossom from there. I bounced around to different jobs, learning everything I could along the way. I have worked in retail, the food service industry, childcare, non-profit organizations, different trades, and more. I have been able to grow my wealth of knowledge from these various positions and have molded that into what I would consider a fortune of real-life experience. I guess I am really a “jack of all trades”, so to speak.
