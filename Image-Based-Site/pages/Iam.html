Who Am I?

I Am AshleyRae. 34 years old with four children, Izzy, Izaac, Ezra, and Zoe. I have lived in McHenry for 6 six years. I grew up in the city of Chicago, Illinois, near Northcenter. I attended Lane Tech High School and was fortunate enough to work at some interesting and unique places. I worked ever since I was 13 and was able to build an extensive resume working under different positions in my teens. Some places I've worked at include:

  • Ironworkers Union - Machinery Movers, Riggers, and Erectors Local 136
  • City Year Chicago
  • Wrigley Field
  • Go Roma Italian Restaurant
  • NBGC - Neighborhood Boys and Girls Club

As I got older, I moved around to different suburbs, getting a grasp on the different areas around Chicago. I lived in Oak Park for several years while attending Triton College in River Grove. I was able to take several credit hours in the Graphic Design program, which started my love for computer arts. After taking some time off from school in 2010, I began working full-time after my childhood home was sold and my immediate family moved out of state. I was determined to build my life around my childhood city, and looked for work wherever I was able.

In 2013, I was working four part time jobs and constantly keeping myself busy. I joined the Ironworkers Union (Local 136) in late 2013 and met my current fiancé in the apprenticeship program. While learning a new trade was fun, it wasn’t my ideal job, and I left to find something I had more passion for. Shortly after leaving the union, I found out I was pregnant with my daughter and my whole life shifted. I found a part time job working for a chocolate store and when my daughter was born, I took time off to stay home with her.

Presently, I am taking part-time online college classes for an Associates In Applied Sciences Degree with a focus in Network Security. I graduate in Spring of 2024 and am hoping to find a job in my field shortly after. I currently am working part-time as a server while maintaining my household. It is busy but I wouldn’t have it any other way.
